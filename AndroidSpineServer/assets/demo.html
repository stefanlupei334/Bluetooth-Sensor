Mindfulness Meditation

Band of interest: Theta

Mindfulness meditation is about focusing on what is happening around you and being aware of your thoughts and feelings during the process of meditating. Your mind should be open to your true feelings – there should be no judging of your thoughts. This is a common practice that Buddhist monks use as a way of self-awakening.

Instructions

  1. Get into a comfortable position and then fix your body still.
  2. Let your eyes fall closed
  3. Focus on your breath
  4. Move your attention to the thoughts in your mind and the sounds and sights surrounding you.
  5. Be aware of your elf and surroundings, try not to analyze or judge anything.

Compassion Meditation

Band of interest: Gamma

Compassion meditation produces a state in which love and compassion permeate the whole mind, with no other consideration, reasoning or discursive thoughts.   Compassion meditation produces a very specific feeling — a caring for the well-being of another living being, independent of approving or disapproving of them, or expecting anything in return.  Some report this feeling as a continuous aha! moment.

Instructions

  1. Get into a comfortable position and then fix your body still.
  2. Let your eyes fall closed
  3. Focus on your breath for a few moments, to get the mind settled.
  4. Progressively cultivate loving-kindness towards:
  1. oneself
  2. a good friend
  3. a "neutral" person
  4. a difficult person
  5. all four of the above equally
  6. and then gradually to all beings
